In Kubernetes, a Job is a controller used for finite tasks. Unlike a Deployment (which keeps a Pod running forever, like a web server or MySQL), a Job runs a Pod until it successfully finishes its task and then stops.

Think of a Job as a "run-to-completion" script.





1. How a Job works
When you create a Job, it starts one or more Pods.

If the Pod succeeds (returns exit code 0), the Job is complete.

If the Pod fails (crashes), the Job will automatically restart it until it succeeds or hits a retry limit.
